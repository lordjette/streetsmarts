Open-source Heatmap for Amenities Nationwide Accessibility

Transforming open-source data to provide impactful data, insightful visualizations, and interactive heatmaps on the availability and accessibility of amenities and services in cities across ASEAN countries

Per Amenity Type Accessibility Score

Project OHANA allows users to visualize amenity accessibility for each amenity categories. Gather insights and data as to which areas lack accessibility to certain types of amenities and services. Currently, Project OHANA contains six categories:

  • Health
  • Education
  • Finance
  • Grocery
  • Security
  • Transportation

From Visualizations to Research Leads

Discover patterns to formulate hypothesis and data-driven research.

From our data, the barangay with the lowest amenity score in Quezon City, PH is Bagong Silangan while the highest is South Triangle.


Bagong Silangan has two distinct geographical features that can explain its low amenity accessibility score: a) its lower portion is lying near the bank of Marikina river making it prone to flooding, and b) 25% of its composition are farmlands.

South Triangle is an area within Quezon City bordered by main roads specifically Quezon Avenue, Timog Avenue, and EDSA jampacked with restaurants and eclectic establishments and also known for its vibrant night life from its upscale cocktail bars, gay bars, and karaoke/live music venues.


It also serves as home for two of the leading local TV networks across Philippines.

What is Project OHANA?

Project OHANA was created to cater to all individuals and organizations (urban planners, government, researchers, etc.) who are engaging with urban planning and development. It aims to promote insight-driven research on the availability and accessibility of amenities to facilitate better urban planning which safeguard citizens’ health and livelihoods, and ensure equal access and promote inclusivity across ASEAN citizens.

Our short-term objectives

  •  To identify areas with opportunities for an amenities-led development
  •  To reduce information failures on amenities and land uses
  •  To promote coordination across various government levels

Our long-term objectives

  •  To facilitate better urban planning that safeguards citizens’ health and livelihoods
  •  To help in ensuring equal accessibility and inclusivity among ASEAN citizens
